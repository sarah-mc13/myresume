1969 Chevrolet Copo Camaro

The Copo Camaro was part of Chevy's special order system to create high power road cars. Its quater-mile time was 13.16 secs with about 110 mph. The 9561 included a 427 ci iron block with 425 HP and 4-speed manual.

1969 Plymouth Barracuda

The Barracuda was know to be the first pony car included a 440 big block in a small chasis. This made it a true drivers car. The '64 to '70 featured a full redesign including a 2in wheel base extension.

1970 AMC Rebel

The 1970 Rebel is a successor to the Rambler. The "Machine" package sported a 390 ci V8 producing 340 brake HP and 340 lb-ft of torque, pushing it to a mid 14-sec quater mile time. It also had a 4 speed manual transisson with a hurst shifter.

1969 Chevrolet Nova

The '68 redesign made the Nova larger for a 396 ci motor in the SS model's making 350 brake HP. Also has a 4 speed manual and 14 inch rally wheels. It was one of the quickest muscle cars of the late 60's.

1969 pontiac GTO Judge

"The Judge" was an options package only available for '69 GTO. the car name is from the TV show Laugh-In. It had a rear spoiler, striped body paint and a choice between 366HP Ram Air III or the 370HP Ram Air IV engine.
